Project

„Staging Memory” examines the absense of context in
contemporary World War Two memorial projects



Data, History, Technology, & Design are all equal parts of its DNA

Next

Anticipated Outcomes

Create an interdisciplinary investigation of existing research regarding World War Two, relying on new technologies to produce new insights about war and peace

Work with the latest technological developments that support new forms of research and further the ability to ask new questions and arrive at new conclusions

Establish a new language of memorialization at the 75th anniversary of the end of World War Two, suitable for 21st century audiences.

Develop a sensory-based and interactive visualization and (re)presentation of World War Two using new technological concepts

Offer an interpretation of what it means to understand and deal with war, pain, loss, memory, and loss of memory.

Create new interactive platforms of experience, to interrupt and personalize the depersonalized nature of war.
